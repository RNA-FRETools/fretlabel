FluorLabel

FluorLabel describes a workflow to label nucleic acids in silico. For this purpose, a number of labeling chemistries are implemented, including standard 3’/5’-end labeling as well as internal labeling via modified uracils/deoxythymines or ethenoadducts on adenines and cytosines (Zhao, Nucleic Acids Res. 2018). The process of building these base-linker fragments is documented in a series of Jupyter notebooks in the section Fragment building

FluorLabel already includes a number of cyanines, Alexa and Atto dyes which can be coupled to an RNA or DNA molecule of interest using a PyMOL Plugin. The Plugin integrates abovementioned coupling chemistries in a GUI such that the biomolecules can be labeled with just a few clicks.
